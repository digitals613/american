Essential Pre-shipping Steps

Take note of the following crucial pre-shipment activities:

  • Take a few pictures: Take dated, time-stamped images of every component of your vehicle with a high-quality digital camera. Take a dated, time-stamped video of the automobile on the day of shipping as well if possible.

  • Double-check your insurance coverage: Cover everything from the vehicle's security to the route travelled and the expected arrival time (Estimated Time of Arrival).

  • Confirm shipping specifics, including how the vehicle will be secured, the route travelled, and the estimated arrival time (Estimated Time of Arrival).

  • Stacked Vehicles: Inquire whether the carrier will be stacking automobiles. If the transporter can handle double-deck loads, insist on yours being delivered on the top rack.

Preparing Your Classic Vehicle for Shipment

  • Go Over the Entire Vehicle: Inspect your car from top to bottom, making a note of what works and what doesn't.

  • Exterior: Examine and mark any external features like as trim, mirrors, hood decorations, and any other connected pieces. Make an inventory of the scrapes and existing paint defects. Examine the chromes and their attachments for any missing or loosened connection pins that keep the chrome in place.

  • Interior:Take photos of the whole interior, beginning with the headliners, overhead lighting, and general condition. Seat adjustments, armrests, dash elements, and instruments should all be checked and tested.

  • Undercarriage:Take notice of the undercarriage. Examine the condition of the brake lines, cables, transmission case, and oil pan. Make a note of any dents, scratches, or other damage that has already occurred.

  • Start the Vehicle: To examine drivability, steering, braking, and handling, start the engine and take a test drive.

Complete inspections to determine the vehicle's general condition before shipping is a good idea. Any damage that occurs during transportation may be inspected and treated with this way.

When your car reaches at its location, be present and check it right away while keeping witnesses. Take note of any damages (if any) and notify the hauler as soon as possible, documenting and filing a claim for the damages.
