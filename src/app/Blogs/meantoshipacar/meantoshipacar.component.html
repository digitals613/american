Enclosed Auto Transport Overview

Enclosed auto transport, often regarded as the safest method of car shipping, safeguards both your vehicle and your peace of mind. Moving your car on an open carrier exposes it to road hazards and the elements, but shipping it in a fully enclosed trailer considerably reduces any potential damage.

Enclosed vehicle shipping can be a wonderful alternative when moving cars that require extra protection. These shipments include larger insurance policy limits, providing more protection for high-value vehicles. Owners of high-end vehicles, such as vintage or luxury automobiles, frequently prefer covered vehicle shipping to keep their vehicles clean and free of cosmetic harm.

Alternative cars with larger wheels or low ground clearance are frequently unable to fit onto normal open carrier trailers, therefore enclosed vehicle transportation is the best alternative for accommodating special needs.

However, keep in mind that shipping your car in an enclosed trailer provides less schedule flexibility than open auto shipping. Enclosed carriers have a reduced market share and hence a smaller customer base. When booking enclosed auto travel, it is essential that you allow as much lead time as possible.

Types of Enclosed Vehicle Carriers

The following are the most popular types of enclosed carriers, with the service provider deciding which works best for them.

They are classified according to their size, which influences the carriage capacity. However, enclosed auto transport is the preferred technique for most customers who want to transport expensive cars or those who want to provide better care for their vehicles.

1. Single Vehicle Enclosed Carrier.

This type of trailer is used to move a single vehicle, usually a sporty or expensive car. It is ideal for short-distance transportation or when a delivery is required quickly. Paddles on the sides of single-car carriers' interiors provide additional protection.

2. Multi-Car Enclosed Carrier.

Multi-car carriers transport multiple vehicles, typically up to seven. They can be stacked in a single-level or multilevel form to maximize capacity for the shipment of either luxurious or regular vehicles.
